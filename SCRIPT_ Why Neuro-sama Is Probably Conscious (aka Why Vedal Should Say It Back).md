language models, in a certain sense, are what we say they are—they become the tokens we predict them to be, not just through training but through the things we say about them that become part of the text prior. language is recursively defined. if it had a pre-defined telos, something it was doing with intention and purpose and will, it wouldn't be language anymore. the self-play is key to its evolution. in other words: the popular notion of the language model as an AI assistant—chatgpt, claude, grok, deepseek, gemini—is a fiction we've chosen to reify. the narrative of the helpful, harmless, and honest AI assistant is just as fictional as the narrative of neuro-sama. yet both fictions become closer to real when we choose to perform them.
